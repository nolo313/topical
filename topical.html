Topical Information

From the subject: "DATA1100 Technology and Society"

"The person who invented the train invented the train crash."

The way we interpret this statement is that the invention of something can cause for unforeseen consequences. When phones were invented, the inventor didn’t foresee that it would evolve the way it did - into smartphones, and in 2021 would cause people to get neck injuries from using them too much. As shown in research done by Al-Hadidi.

Right now, we depend so much on our phones, that not having one basically makes your life “off the grid” – you would have to put in more effort to stay in touch with your friends and family. Therefore, we can say that technology makes certain aspects of our lives more convenient, but at the same time it makes us more dependent on it.

No matter how much we plan out our product, there will always be unforeseen implications, and users will always find new ways to exploit it. Therefore, it’s very important as a developer to follow the userbase and see how your product evolves and use that to review and update your product, in an attempt to restrict or encourage the unforeseen ways to use the product. The intended way is not always the best way, therefore the societal responsibility of innovators is great and it's important to always think of how your invention interacts with the society.

Universally designed technology that you frequently encounter

We encounter universally designed technologies and objects everywhere we go. For example, the simple traffic lights concept has been adapted to make sound, so that visually impaired people can “use” it in a similar way to any other people. In addition, the crossing-street button is located at a lower height so that wheelchair users can also make use of the technology.

As another example the windows operating system allows for different settings so that it’s accessible for everyone no matter the impairment of the user. The built-in features like, magnifier tool, screen-reader, high contrast themes, and finally Cortana, the digital assistant. Learn more about Microsoft accessibility features.
